A Week in the Islands: Sample BVI Itinerary

Every week is a little different based on weather and the group, but here’s a snapshot of what a father–son sailing adventure can look like.

Day 1 – Welcome Aboard (Tortola)

Board your catamaran, get settled into your cabin, and meet the other dads and sons. Safety briefing and intro to sailing basics.

Evening devotion: “Leaving the dock” — choosing to be fully present this week.

Day 2 – The Baths & First Snorkel

Explore the giant boulders and hidden pools at The Baths, then snorkel in clear water and grab beach time together.

Conversation: What I see in you, son / what I admire in you, Dad.

Day 3 – Island Hopping & Kayak Races

Learn more about sailing, take the helm, and join kayak or paddleboard races between boats. Watch the sunset from the trampoline and stargaze from the bow.

Day 4 – Anegada Adventure & Setup for Treasure Hunt

Long blue-water sail to Anegada. Beach time, optional fishing, and lobster on the beach.

Devotion: Anchors and roots — what holds your life steady.

Day 5 – Nautical Treasure Hunt (Anegada to Sandy Cay)

Deep-water sail with a nautical treasure hunt. Use charts, bearings, and GPS clues to uncover “hidden” spots like the cannons off Saba. Knot-tying challenges where each knot mastered unlocks another clue.

Day 6 – Bioluminescent Swim & Beach Fire

Snorkeling and beach time during the day. At night (conditions allowing), slip into glowing water for a bioluminescent swim, followed by a beach fire.

Dad’s blessing: guided time for fathers to speak blessing and prayer over their sons.

Day 7 – Final Sail & Sending Out

Easy morning sail back toward base. Closing worship and reflection: how to bring what you’ve experienced home. Goodbyes… and plans for the next trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What age range is this for?

Generally, we recommend sons from age 11–18. If your son is slightly younger or older, let’s talk. The key is that both of you are ready to engage, help on the boat, and participate in group activities.

Do I need sailing experience?

No. Many dads and sons come with zero sailing background. Our skippers handle the hard stuff and happily teach anyone who wants to learn.

Is this a Christian trip?

Yes. We openly center our week on following Jesus. You can expect daily Scripture, prayer, worship, and honest conversations about faith—without pressure or performance.

What if I get seasick?

The BVI are known for relatively calm, protected waters and short hops between islands. We still recommend bringing motion sickness remedies that work for you, and we’ll talk through best practices before you come.

What’s the payment and refund policy?

We’ll review specific terms before you register, including deposit amounts, final payment due dates, and refund timelines if plans change or if there are weather or travel disruptions.
